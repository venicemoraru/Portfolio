TAR
short story
2026

The confrontation with a can of tar, its toxicity and its beauty, becomes unexpectedly personal.

The absurdity of the situation was overwhelming. I stared at the tin can restlessly, the shifting of my body fuelled less by the fear of its contents than by my own inability to predict what I would do next. The creaking floor had no effect on the can's black liquid. Its lustrous surface remained immune to the bend and strain of the floorboards beneath it, and its ability to reflect the room's silence was as strong as its ability to reflect its light.

An impermeable fullness moulded itself around everything within the space: a quiet so thick that it blurred the edges between objects, converting the passing of time into something physical. My body itched in anticipation, agitation followed the push and pull of the monologue inside my head.

A glance towards the can's warning label spurred my turmoil—its appeal to good conscience had me fear a growing temptation I could not overlook. I did not, however, need the warning label to understand the liquid's danger. Its darkness, its story, were cautionary enough.

My heart beat faster and my mouth grew wet as I measured the substance's viscosity with my eyes. Trying to control myself, I omitted the can from my line of sight. Its gleam, however, found a way into my peripheral no matter where I looked. The temptation was ridiculous, almost comical. In both amusement and embarrassment, I drew in a sharp breath—though was halted by what had entered my body.

The scent entered my face and lingered in my sinuses. Exhalation felt sacrilege—I could not bear to lose this tinted air. The burn I expected was countered with a surprising softness, and the dulcet notes of the tar's scent pacified the shock brought by broken anticipation.

My mind, though, soon began to question what my body had initially accepted. Trust in the liquid's fumes quickly dissipated along with the trust I had in myself. Fear arose and my senses dimmed, warnings of poison ground my mind. I was told the stories, I had studied the hazard—and no amount of personal evidence could have me disregard the information that had formed my initial judgment: the liquid and its fumes were dangerous.

Despite the force with which the air left my lungs, the fumes' unexpected delicacy shocked me yet again. The exhalation was fresh; it cooled my nose and tongue. My oesophagus was soothed by its makeup and a taste of medicine coated my teeth. I tried to follow its invisible form, the elegant folds and ribbons grazing the bow of my upper lip, only to confront the stare of desperate eyes in the dark reflection before me.

What I first saw to be a look of weakness soon turned to one of humility. The liquid remained unmoved by the turbulence it brought me, and its power grew all the more dominant. I tried to unweave the contradiction that was revealed, in an attempt to establish some peace. But the attention only deepened interest.

Questions strained me—how something so lethal could have treated me so gently. Perhaps I have in fact been poisoned, now foolishly awaiting the inevitable destiny of my own making. After all: the liquid and its fumes were dangerous.

But my gut could not forget the innocence. The beauty. And the fear I once felt so heavy in my stomach was now localised to my head. The conflict became a mental one, and only deepened by my body's growing trust in the liquid. It searched for freedom from the terror that controlled my mind, through its own calculated sentience. And in a moment between circulating thoughts, my body escaped.

Numb fingers freed themselves and found peace in the air. I watched in horror as they approached the tin can ahead. Though perhaps as an excuse to indulge, I accepted the loss of control.

My fingertip grazed its surface, and the liquid seemed to accept my touch the way my body had its fumes. A light pull guided me deeper into the can, and the tar moulded itself around my hand, as though to hold it. Its coolness came with warmth; a kind one. My lips vibrated and my eyes grew hot as nameless emotions ran through me. I squeezed my hand and the tar squeezed back: the reciprocity made clear the truth that my body came to know.

The darkness I had been taught to fear was not one of evil: it was one of shelter. Of ease, of love. And my acceptance of the tar was ultimately the acceptance of something unexpectedly personal.

A trembling hand brought the liquid to my cheek. Smeared, it held me. Its perfume entered my nose, with familiar grace. Fingers slid across my face and the liquid traced my profile. And at once, my mind found silence.

The tar lacquered my lips and polished my teeth. Its glossy folds covered my palate and caressed my tongue, filling every crevice within my mouth. It was delicious. Comfort and sweetness were heightened with acidity and spice into a flavour that was anything but toxic. The complexity of aromas came together into something beautiful.

As it filled my stomach, the liquid ignited light. It was vitamin; fuel. My heart beat with enthusiasm and my blood ran with vigour. My lungs surpassed their known capacity, and I revelled in the taste of life that lingered in my mouth.

And the tar found itself within me. A union so seamless it would have been absurd to have ever questioned its integrity. But with the liquid now integrated into my matter, the bottom of the can seemed to show more than just my reflection. A clarity that surpassed its solidity. A darkness that glowed with light. And ultimately, a black smear, of what remained of the tar.
